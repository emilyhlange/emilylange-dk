Pornolampen

Year — 2021 Type — 3D Design / Spatial

An interactive lamp that illuminates when a resident of a student dormitory visits a pornographic website — designed to de-stigmatise pornography and open conditions for conversation.

The Concept

Pornolampen was designed for placement in a shared common area of a student dormitory — a kitchen, a living room. When any resident on the shared WiFi visits a pornographic website, the lamp turns on. It does not reveal who — only that someone, somewhere in the building, is watching. Visibility without identity.

The design emerges from a simple question: can an object make a taboo subject easier to discuss? Not by forcing confrontation, but by making the private quietly legible in a shared space — and letting people decide whether to respond.

Design Manifesto

We want to offer users a safe and free space to talk about pornography — to feel comfortable discussing something so private. We will not force users into a conversation they don't want; rather, we create the conditions for one. This space could change the way we talk about pornography and our relationship to sexuality, desire, and the body. Does the project aim to provoke? Yes — and also: to open.

Technical Implementation

The lamp is controlled by an Arduino and Processing system. A Processing sketch monitors the connected computer's network traffic for Pornhub; when a visit is detected, it sends a signal to an Arduino board. A relay module bridges the 5V Arduino to the 230V lamp circuit, allowing the board to switch the light. The lamp is on while the site is visited; off when the session ends.
